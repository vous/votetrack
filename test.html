111th Congress

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

Mr. EVERETT of MASSACHUSETTS for himself and with Speaker CLOUSEAU of WISCONSIN, Mr. LEWESA of VIRGINIA, Mr. REYNOLDS of OHIO, Mr. ALLEN of VIRGINIA, and Mr. PRESCOTT of NEW YORK, for themselves and for others offers -

A BILL


To repeal the Defense of Marriage Act and to amend the United States Code of Military Justice to repeal the "Don't Ask, Don't Tell" Policy.


Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,

Sec. I. Short Title.

    This bill may be referred to as the "Federal Same Sex Marriage Recognition Act of 2010."


Sec. II. Findings.

    1. Whereas it is estimated that approximately 8 Million adult American men and women identify as members of the LGBT Community;
    2. Whereas this number include 4 Million identifying as Gay or Lesbian, and an additional 4 Million identifying as Bisexual;
    3. Noting that there are several states and regions throughout the nation which attempt to deny LGBT couples the right to marry their partner;
    4. Stressing that the First Amendment's Freedom of Religion clause also allows the freedom from religion for those who might be persecuted due to their livelihoods;
    5. Dismayed that there is a continual effort in this nation to discredit and defame members of the LGBT Community;
    6. Alarmed at the amount of intolerance felt towards members of the LGBT exemplified in the recent hate crime attack in Jackson, Misssissippi;
    7. Concerned for the future of LGBT youths and young adults while such acts of violence continue to occur;

Sec. III. Provisions.

    1. 1 U.S. Code § 7 is amended to read as follows:
        In determining the meaning of any Act of Congress, or of any ruling, regulation, or interpretation of the various administrative bureaus and agencies of the United States, the word “marriage” means a legal union between two persons who have been joined in matrimony, and the word “spouse” refers only to a person who is a husband or a wife of any person of any gender.
    2. Chapter 115 of title 28, United States Code is amended by striking section 1738C in its entirety;
    3. Chapter 37 of title 10, United States Code is amended by striking section 654. in its entirety;

Sec. IV. Enactment.

    The provisions of this bill shall take effect upon its constitutional passage.

Repeals the Defense of Marriage Act
Repeals "Don't Ask, Don't Tell"
